🚀 Getting Started

How do I access the admin panel?▼
Go to Firebase Console → Firestore → users collection → find your document → change the role field from "student" to "admin". Log out and back in — you'll be redirected to the admin dashboard automatically.
How do students register?▼
Students register themselves on the login page using email/password or Google sign-in. They're automatically added to Firestore with role "student". They complete their profile (roll number, branch) in the Settings page.
How do I set up email notifications?▼
Edit the backend/.env file: set SMTP_USER to your Gmail address and SMTP_PASS to your Gmail App Password. To get an App Password: Google Account → Security → Enable 2FA → App Passwords → Generate. Restart the backend after saving.

📋 Reviewing Essays

What is the review workflow?▼
1. Student submits essay → 2. AI scores automatically (Gemini + NLP) → 3. Appears in your Check Assessments queue → 4. You review and submit your score + feedback → 5. Comparison report generated → 6. Student sees both scores in their dashboard.
Can I edit a review after submitting?▼
Currently reviews cannot be edited through the UI. If needed, directly edit the admin_reviews document in Firebase Console. A future update will add edit functionality.
What happens after I submit a review?▼
The system saves your score and feedback, updates the assessment status to "reviewed", calls the backend to generate an admin report and comparison report via Gemini AI, then updates the reports collection. Students can immediately see both scores and the comparison.

🤖 AI Scoring System

How does the AI score essays?▼
Two layers: (1) NLTK extracts structural features — word count, vocabulary richness (TTR), sentence variety, paragraph count, transition word usage. (2) Google Gemini 1.5 Flash scores 5 dimensions: Content & Relevance (0-20), Structure (0-20), Language (0-20), Grammar (0-20), Critical Thinking (0-20). Total = 100.
What if the AI backend is offline?▼
Essays are saved to Firestore with status "pending" and ai_score: null. You can review them manually through Check Assessments. When the backend comes back online, re-trigger scoring by POSTing to /score with the assessment_id, topic, and essay.
How is the random topic generated?▼
Topics are generated client-side from a bank of 8 domains (CS, Electronics, Mechanical, Civil, Biotech, IT, Environment, General), each with 10 curated essay topics. Math.random() selects one when the student picks a domain. Students can regenerate before accepting.
